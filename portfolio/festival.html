Western Visayas Festivals

Click a card to explore each festival

Ati-Atihan
Aklan
When: Every 3rd week of January The Ati-Atihan Festival is one of the oldest and most famous festivals in the Philippines. People paint their faces, wear tribal costumes, and dance in the streets to honor the Santo Niño. It is full of music, faith, and energy, making Kalibo very lively during the celebration.
Dinagyang
Iloilo
When: Every 4th Sunday of January The Dinagyang Festival is a religious and cultural celebration honoring the Santo Niño. It features powerful street dancing, drum beats, and colorful costumes that tell stories of devotion and history.
Paraw Regatta
Iloilo
Every February This is Asia’s oldest traditional sailboat race. It showcases colorful sailboats called “paraws” racing across the Iloilo Strait, celebrating the city’s strong connection to the sea.
MassKara
Bacolod
Every October (3rd week of October) Famous for its smiling masks, this festival symbolizes hope and resilience. It is one of the biggest and most colorful festivals in the Philippines.
Manggahan
Guimaras
Every May (usually May 11–22) This festival celebrates Guimaras’ world-famous sweet mangoes. It includes street dancing, trade fairs, and food exhibitions.
Panaad
Negros Occidental
Every April A grand celebration where all cities and towns of Negros Occidental showcase their culture, products, and traditions.
Binirayan
Antique
Every December (around Dec 1–12) This festival celebrates the legendary arrival of the Malay settlers in Panay Island. It includes cultural performances, historical reenactments, and parades.
Sinadya sa Halaran
Capiz
Every January (during Dinagyang season) A “festival of festivals” where different cultural groups from Panay Island compete and showcase their traditions.
Halaran
Capiz
When: December (combined with Sinadya sa Halaran in modern celebrations) A cultural celebration that honors the history and traditions of Capiz.
Kasadyahan
Iloilo
When: Every January (during Dinagyang season) A “festival of festivals” where different cultural groups from Panay Island compete and showcase their traditions.
Hugyaw Kansilay
Silay
Every June This festival highlights the history and cultural heritage of Silay City through performances and storytelling.
Sagingan
Antique
When: Varies per municipality (usually harvest season, around March–May) A local festival that highlights banana production and rural life in Antique.
